UX/UI DESIGN PROCESS

Something good - Web platform for posting and getting information about volunteering events

Idea

The initial idea was to build a web application that would promote community engagement by creating a platform where people can post and get information about volunteering events in Northern Israel. The volunteering events would be based on a one time commitment in a variety of activity-types and locations. Users would be able to vote for the volunteering event they like, and each week, one of the events will take place at the time and location that was set.

Research

We chose the informal method for our research; We checked the alternatives and found out that there is no web app similar to our idea in the way that volunteers do not have to commit for more than a one time event and that other platforms offer organizations to post their events, but private users do not have that opportunity. User Personas

The main user persona we defined - Johnny:

  • Age: 45
  • Residency: Northern Israel
  • Family Status: Married + 3
  • Profession: Banker
  • Hobbies: Playstation, basketball, community events
  • Cares about: His family, his town

Wireframe

The initial sketches include a login page, voting page for upcoming events, a page for the main event of the coming week, ways to help through donations and participation, form to add an event, thank you page, and a page to share the event with others:

This idea changed a lot after the first user testing and in the process of creating the proptotype.

Prototype

We created the prototype in both Figma and InVision prototyping tools. The goals for the prototype were to understand and figur the design and flow of the app and at the same time to create a tool that would allow user testing Screenshots from the prototype:

User Testing

We conducted user testing with people that matched the audience we were aiming the app for. We interviewed potential users and gave them the opportunity to interact with the app. We took notes and made significant changes according to the results, including changing the placement of certain features and removing menu items that were not intuitive (as the voting page).
